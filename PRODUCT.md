# PRODUCT.md — upDev / Cracked Developer HQ

register: product

## Product purpose

A personal engineering OS for exactly one user: Collins, a senior TypeScript
full-stack engineer transitioning into AI + Cloud engineering over a 12-month
roadmap (3 tracks, 5 phases, 17 skills, 6 projects, weekly sprints, career
board). Opened daily for 2–10 minute focused sessions, morning and night, to
answer one question instantly: "where am I, and what do I do next?"

## Users

One user, forever. Solo and private, never multi-tenant. He is fluent in the
best-designed developer tools (Linear, Raycast, Vercel, Arc) and will notice
every subtly-off component. The interface must be the reason he opens the app;
motivation and momentum are product features.

## Brand / tone

Serious instrument, not a toy. Distinctive, modern, sleek, effortless.
High information density handled with restraint. A point of view, not a
mashup of references. The vertical progress spine is the app's identity:
it should be the screenshot.

## Anti-references (what this must NOT read as)

- Generic developer-dark-mode / Vercel-template clone
- Neon-on-black "gamer" or crypto dashboards
- SaaS hero-metric dashboards with identical card grids
- Sparse-to-useless minimalism
- The current "warm terminal / graph paper" look is the incumbent to beat:
  competent, but it is the second-order category reflex.

## Strategic principles

- Typography is the backbone; a real type system, not Inter at five sizes.
- Color is surgical: accents carry meaning (status, track), never decoration.
- Motion conveys state; fast, physical, exponential ease-out; full
  reduced-motion support.
- One signature element done exceptionally beats ten decorations.
- Accessibility is non-negotiable: real contrast, focus states, keyboard nav.
- Fully responsive; must be genuinely usable on a phone.

## Constraints

Next.js App Router + TypeScript strict + Tailwind v4 + ShadCN + Drizzle.
Redesign is presentation-layer only: keep all routes, functionality, data
layer, and server actions.
